About

I am an Associate Professor and Director of Graduate Studies in the Department of Political Science at the University of Florida. My research examines the intersection of race and racism in international politics, global inequality, migration policy, forced displacement, and international order. My work has appeared in the American Journal of Political Science, Political Analysis, International Studies Quarterly, Journal of Peace Research, and other journals.

My first book, Undesirable Immigrants: Why Racism Persists in International Migration (Princeton University Press, 2022), received the 2023 Best Book Award from the Race, Ethnicity, and Politics section of the American Political Science Association. I am writing a second book, Hierarchies of Worth: Race and the Politics of Costly State Action.

I received my Ph.D. from Ohio State University, an M.Sc. (Research) in International Relations from the London School of Economics, and a B.A. in International Studies and Political Science from Johns Hopkins University. I grew up in Des Moines, Iowa.
